# Carry-Over System Definition Statement

**Produced by:** DDA Rerun Assessment — Run 1
**Source run:** DDA_Run_WestAfricaRegionalElectricityGrid_2026-03-31_1127.md
**Date:** 2026-03-31
**Primary uncertainty this run is designed to resolve:** Whether any existing actor or actor-set within the WAPP regional coordination layer has or can be given binding authority to operate a pre-breach callable-generation adequacy gate across member-state operating decisions — and if not, what institutional arrangement would be required before that authority is reachable in practice.

---

## Suggested System Definition Statement

**System:** West Africa regional electricity grid — regional coordination and dispatch layer

**Boundary:** Includes the regional coordination body (WAPP), cross-border dispatch arrangements, interconnection operating agreements, member-state system operators in their regional coordination capacity, and any operating instruments, rule sets, or binding procedures governing callable-generation availability decisions at the regional layer. Excludes generation assets below the regional dispatch layer, transmission infrastructure, upstream fuel supply chains, downstream end-user consumption, national energy policy and regulatory bodies, and financing and procurement functions.

**Failure Condition:** The regional coordination and dispatch layer cannot enforce a binding callable-generation adequacy boundary before regional usable callable generation falls below the level required to sustain load — evidenced by the absence of a pre-breach regional stop point, open override routes between member-state discretion and the required regional gate, or no evidenced actor with binding authority to refuse or reverse availability-reducing decisions across the regional operating boundary.

**Time Horizon:** 18–36 months

---

## Rationale for scope change

Run 1 identified the required regional callable-generation adequacy gate as not evidenced and non-binding, with the required actor reachability remaining Unknown across the full regional boundary. The most important unresolved question is whether the WAPP regional coordination layer has or could have binding authority to operate a pre-breach adequacy gate — a question that the broader regional grid boundary in Run 1 could not resolve through structural reasoning alone. Tightening the boundary to the regional coordination and dispatch layer focuses the next run on the institutional and authority architecture where that question is most directly testable.

---

## Residual uncertainties carried forward from prior run

1. Whether the required regional callable-generation adequacy gate has never been built in binding form, or whether it existed and has migrated downstream — the exact balance between downstream mislocation and never-built architecture remains unresolved.
2. Whether any existing actor or actor-set has binding authority to refuse, defer, or reverse availability-reducing decisions across the regional operating boundary before the callable-generation adequacy floor is breached.
3. Whether the trajectory of the denial architecture is worsening, stable at a structurally weak level, or recovering — no time-sequenced evidence was available on the current record.
4. Whether callable-generation shortfall is primarily a true regional adequacy-control failure or a nominal-capacity illusion / transmission-isolation problem that leaves available generation non-usable at the regional operating layer.
5. The co-binding condition: frequency-control reserve margin remains the strongest alternative scarcity at confidence 7/10 — the next run should remain alert to whether the authority gap maps more directly onto stability-layer mechanisms or volume-layer mechanisms.
6. All findings from Run 1 carry reduced confidence: the evidence base was predominantly inferred. A field-informed rerun using the Evidence-informed model is required before findings are treated as operationally validated.
